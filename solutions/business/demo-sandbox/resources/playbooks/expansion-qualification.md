# Playbook — Expansion qualification

Apply when a customer is in **expansion_opportunity** status (typically:
seat utilization > 90% sustained for 60+ days, OR exec sponsor brings up
adjacent teams).

## Qualification checklist

Before pitching an upsell, confirm all four:

- [ ] **Sustained usage at cap** — utilization has been at-or-near 100%
  for two months, not a one-month blip
- [ ] **Internal champion** — someone customer-side has built or asked
  for the ROI case. If you have to build it for them from scratch, this
  is early-stage qualification, not active expansion
- [ ] **Buyer access** — the person who can sign the expansion contract
  has been identified, even if you haven't met them yet
- [ ] **No competitive bake-off active** — if they're also evaluating
  alternatives, the expansion talk needs to wait until that's resolved
  (or you'll lose both)

## Pricing strategy

- **Tier-up before seat-add**: if their seat-add would put them within
  reach of the next tier, propose the tier — feature unlock plus
  per-seat math usually nets better than incremental seats at the
  current tier
- **Multi-year for material discount only**: trade discount for term
  length, never the reverse
- **Don't anchor on per-seat**: lead with platform value and ROI of the
  expanded use case, not "you're paying X per seat now, this would be Y"

## Timing

- **Quote within 5 business days** of internal qualification — momentum
  matters; champion's attention is finite
- **Close within 45 days** of quote — if procurement drags past that,
  re-qualify (something changed)

## Red flags that look like expansion but aren't

- Champion asks for a quote but hasn't talked to their boss
- Heavy usage from a 30-day spike (often a one-off project, not
  steady-state)
- "Sponsor" who's actually a junior IC enthusiastic about the product —
  great future champion, not a buyer today
