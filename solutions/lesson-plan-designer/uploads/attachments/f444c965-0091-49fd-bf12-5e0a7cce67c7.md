# Study Guide: Addition and Subtraction of Algebraic Expressions

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the principles and operations involved in the addition and subtraction of algebraic expressions (integral expressions), specifically focusing on the concepts of like terms, the laws for combining them, and the rules for removing parentheses.

## I. Fundamental Concepts

### 1. Definition of Integral Expressions (整式)
Algebraic expressions are categorized into two primary forms:
*   **Monomials**: The product of numbers and letters (e.g., $3x$, $-2ab^2$).
*   **Polynomials**: The sum of several monomials (e.g., $2x + 3y - 5$).

### 2. Like Terms (同类项)
Terms are considered "like terms" if they satisfy two specific conditions:
1.  **Same Letters**: They must contain the exact same variables.
2.  **Same Indices**: The exponents of the corresponding letters must be identical.

**Key Characteristics of Like Terms:**
*   They are independent of the coefficient (the numerical factor).
*   They are independent of the order in which the letters are written (e.g., $3x^2y$ and $-5yx^2$ are like terms).
*   Constant terms (pure numbers) are always considered like terms with each other (e.g., $5$ and $-8$).

## II. Core Operational Laws

### 1. Combining Like Terms
The law for combining like terms dictates that you add the coefficients of the terms while keeping the letters and their respective exponents unchanged.

**Operational Steps:**
1.  Identify the like terms within the expression.
2.  Sum the coefficients of those like terms.
3.  Append the original letters and indices to the new coefficient.

### 2. Removing Parentheses (去括号)
The rules for removing parentheses depend entirely on the sign preceding the bracket:

| Sign Before Parentheses | Effect on Internal Terms | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Plus Sign (+)** | The signs of all terms inside remain **unchanged**. | $a + (b + c) = a + b + c$ |
| **Minus Sign (-)** | The signs of **every** term inside must be **reversed**. | $a - (b + c) = a - b - c$ |

**Mnemonic Strategy**: "Plus sign means no change; minus sign means all change."

## III. Standard Procedure for Simplification

To perform the addition and subtraction of algebraic expressions accurately, follow these sequential steps:
1.  **Remove Parentheses**: Use the positive/negative rules to clear brackets. If nested brackets (like square brackets) exist, work from the inside out.
2.  **Identify Like Terms**: Locate terms with matching variables and exponents.
3.  **Combine**: Sum the coefficients and write the simplified expression.

## IV. Error Analysis: Common Pitfalls
When performing these operations, awareness of the following frequent mistakes is essential for accuracy:

*   **Sign Reversal Failures**: Forgetting to change the signs of *all* terms inside a bracket when a minus sign precedes it (e.g., mistakenly writing $a - (b - c)$ as $a - b - c$ instead of $a - b + c$).
*   **Incorrect Index Modification**: Attempting to add the exponents when combining terms (e.g., incorrectly stating $2x^2 + 3x^2 = 5x^4$ instead of $5x^2$).
*   **Merging Non-Like Terms**: Attempting to combine terms with different variables or exponents (e.g., trying to simplify $2x + 3y$ into $5xy$).

## VII. Glossary of Important Terms

*   **Monomial (单项式)**: A mathematical expression consisting of a single term that is a product of numbers and variables.
*   **Polynomial (多项式)**: An algebraic expression that is the sum of two or more monomials.
*   **Integral Expression (整式)**: The collective term for monomials and polynomials.
*   **Like Terms (同类项)**: Terms within an expression that have the same variables raised to the same powers.
*   **Coefficient (系数)**: The numerical factor that multiplies the variables in a term.
*   **Combining Like Terms (合并同类项)**: The process of simplifying an expression by adding or subtracting the coefficients of terms that have identical variable parts.
*   **Removing Parentheses (去括号)**: The process of eliminating brackets from an expression by applying the distributive property of the positive or negative sign preceding them.
